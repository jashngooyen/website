ASSIGNMENT: Design a modular railing to accommodate for a flexible set.

     For the Drama Dawg Theater Company's 2016 production of "Dirty Rotten Scoundrels", I was designated to bring the conceptual modular railing design to life.

     Each 4x3' foam piece for the railing required an hour each with a jig saw to remove all of the negative space in the design, leading to a total of 10 hours simply sawing foam. The foam removed from the original 4x8' foam sheets to create the tall arches on the platform was used to create the railing, maximizing material efficiency.

 

     To create the modular design itself, wooden frames were designed to go one foot into the large platform, stopped by a lateral 2x4". To accommodate for the 2x4"s making up the plaforms for the main set, the inner slots for the outer removable-railing pieces were offset 2" from the edge of the platform. Said slots were also cut by a jig saw and sanded accordingly to facilitate the movement of the railing in the slot itself.
