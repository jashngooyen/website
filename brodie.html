"The Prime of Miss Jean Brodie"

ASSIGNMENT: Compose original music to be used in UIL One-Act Play.

     In order to avoid the new licensing and copyright rules posed by UIL in 2016, I was assigned to compose original music for the Drama Dawg Theater Company's production of "The Prime of Miss Jean Brodie" for UIL One-Act Play. I had originally proposed to play the music live, but due to scheduling conflicts with events happening in both Band and Winter Guard on the same day, synthesized music was to be played instead.

Composing

     It proved helpful that I had partaken in the Lone Star Youth Wind Orchestra during the 2016 season. For the first concert, we prepared a completely Celtic program. Thus made me more familiar with the sound of Celtic music. Then, I researched the music theory behind Celtic music and came across a webpage that gave me the knowledge to begin composing. Mainly, the information that I needed to compose Celtic music was:

 

"use of the mixolydian and dorian modes"

"pentatonic scales"

"irish flute or irish fiddle"

 

     With these aspects in mind, I whipped up two pieces for use in transitions: one at a moderate tempo, and one at a slower tempo.
